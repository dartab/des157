audience test results

During my audience tests, I saw how three different people interacted with my project, navigated through the site, and reacted to the content. I gained helpful insight in how to structure the site hierarchically and also as to what information is missing conceptually to better communicate what the project is about.

Here are some specific learnings that I will modify for the final delivery:

  1. There needs to a a consistent floating logo in the top left that navigates back to the homepage
  2. There needs to a a consistent floating logo in the top left that navigates back to the homepage
  3. More details needed for “about” section - likely will need a separate page - specifically, why do all these elements belong together?
  4. On the homepage, there should be a tagline or explanation front and center explaining what this is
  5. The “upcoming” page needs more explanation, specifics of file types and the story and concept behind the upcoming zine
  6. Submissions should go on the same page as “upcoming,” as in its current state it appears to be done and in progress
  7. If an issue is complete and in process, that could go on the homepage
  8. Less pages are better
  9. Title should be explained somewhere on the site, perhaps in “about” section
